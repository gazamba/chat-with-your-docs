# The Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China is a series of fortifications built across the historical
northern borders of ancient Chinese states and Imperial China. Its total length,
including all branches, is about 21,196 kilometres.

The wall was built and rebuilt over many centuries. The best-known sections were
constructed by the Ming dynasty between 1368 and 1644. It was built primarily to
protect Chinese states against raids and invasions from nomadic groups of the
Eurasian Steppe.

Contrary to a popular myth, the Great Wall is not visible to the naked eye from
space. It was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1987.
