# The Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is a wrought-iron lattice tower on the Champ de Mars in Paris,
France. It is 330 metres tall and was completed in 1889 as the entrance arch to
the 1889 World's Fair.

The tower was designed by the engineer Gustave Eiffel, whose company built it
between 1887 and 1889. For 41 years it was the tallest man-made structure in the
world, until the Chrysler Building in New York was finished in 1930.

The tower has three levels open to visitors. Tickets can be purchased to ascend
by stairs or lift to the first and second levels. The walk from ground level to
the first level is over 300 steps.
